Rutgers University–New Brunswick: Location, Contact, and Mission

Location: Rutgers University–New Brunswick is located in the heart of central New Jersey. Its main address is 7 College Ave, New Brunswick, NJ 08901, USA. The campus is known for its historic roots and vibrant academic community.

Mission and Values:
Rutgers University–New Brunswick is dedicated to providing a world-class education while fostering an inclusive environment for all students. Its missions include:

  • Advancing knowledge through innovative research and scholarly activities.
  • Offering high-quality education that prepares students for leadership roles in a global society.
  • Promoting public service and engagement with local and global communities.
  • Championing diversity, equity, and inclusion in all aspects of campus life.

Academics at Rutgers University–New Brunswick

Top Majors and Programs:
Rutgers University–New Brunswick offers a diverse range of programs, with top majors including:

  • Business
  • Computer Science
  • Engineering
  • Biological Sciences
  • Psychology
  • Pharmacy
  • Political Science

Curriculum Overview:
Students are required to complete general education classes, which include courses in writing, math, social sciences, and natural sciences. In addition to these, each major has its own set of core courses.

Faculty Expertise:
Rutgers–New Brunswick faculty are leaders in their fields, including renowned experts in healthcare policy, molecular biology, and artificial intelligence. Faculty members are actively engaged in cutting-edge research and mentorship.

Student Life

Facilities and Amenities: Rutgers–New Brunswick offers state-of-the-art campus facilities and amenities, including modern libraries, athletic centers, student lounges, and extensive dining options.

Student Organizations and Clubs: The university is home to over 500 student organizations and clubs, ranging from academic groups to cultural, recreational, and service-oriented clubs, providing a vibrant student life.

Social and extracurricular activities: Social and extracurricular activities are abundant at Rutgers–New Brunswick, with frequent campus events, concerts, workshops, and festivals that foster a close-knit community.

Housing options and cost: Housing options at Rutgers–New Brunswick include traditional dormitories, suite-style residences, and apartments. The cost of on-campus housing varies, with traditional dorms ranging from $8,557 to $9,855 per year, while suites and apartments can range from $9,300 to $11,000, depending on the residence hall and room type.

Tuition and Financial Aid Information

Tuition and Fees: For the 2023-2024 academic year, the in-state tuition for full-time undergraduate students is approximately $15,804, while out-of-state students pay around $33,005.

Financial Aid Options: Rutgers offers various financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, and loans. Students may apply for need-based aid through the FAFSA, which can qualify them for federal and state grants. Rutgers also offers merit-based scholarships for academic excellence.

Payment Plans: Rutgers provides a payment plan option that allows students to spread their tuition payments over several months. Plans can be set up for 4, 5, or 6 installments depending on when they are initiated.

Deadlines: Financial aid application deadlines typically fall in the spring for the upcoming academic year, and tuition payment deadlines vary by semester.
